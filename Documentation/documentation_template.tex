\section{Introduction}

This document outlines a project that explores the intersection of human movement and musical expression. The primary objective is to develop an interactive system that translates a user's physical movements into musical notes played on a digital pipe organ. This project can be broadly divided into two key stages:

\begin{itemize}
	\item Capturing and Interpreting of Movements
	\item Transmission of Motion Data to Digital Pipe Organ as MIDI Signal
\end{itemize}

\section{Elaboration on Stages}

\subsection{Capturing and Interpreting of Movements}

\subsubsection{Kinect}

The project initially explored the use of the Microsoft Kinect device for capturing and interpreting user movements. The Kinect offered several advantages:

\begin{itemize} 
\item 3D Data Capture: The Kinect's dual camera system and various sensors allowed for capturing 3D positional data of the user's body, providing rich information for movement analysis.
\end{itemize}

\noindent However, significant challenges arose during development:
\begin{itemize}

\item Limited Compatibility: The Kinect device's reliance on Windows operating systems posed an obstacle for team members using different platforms.
\item Accessibility Issues: The discontinuation of Kinect production made acquiring the device difficult. The team faced limitations in providing device support for concurrent testing across multiple team members. This significantly hampered development speed.
\end{itemize}

\noindent\underline{Outcome and Decision:} \\
Due to the accessibility limitations for both the development team and potential users, the team decided to investigate more modern, dynamic, and user-friendly solutions for capturing and interpreting movements.\\

\subsubsection{Leveraging Machine Learning: Webcam-based Pose Estimation with MoveNet}

Following the exploration of the Kinect device, the project pivoted towards investigating the capabilities of artificial intelligence-supported models for capturing and interpreting movements. This shift offered several advantages:

\begin{itemize}
\item Accessibility: Webcams are readily available on most personal computers, eliminating the need for additional sensors or specialized equipment. This significantly reduces barriers to both development and user adoption.
\item Cross-platform Compatibility: Webcam functionality is inherent to most operating systems, ensuring broader compatibility compared to the Windows-specific Kinect.
\item Development Efficiency: The widespread availability of webcams allows each team member to conduct independent testing and development cycles, accelerating the iterative process.
Given these advantages, the team decided to explore MoveNet's Lightning model as the first approach. This selection leveraged the TensorFlow and OpenPose libraries, offering a well-established framework for pose estimation tasks.
\end{itemize}

While the initial tests with MoveNet demonstrated promising results in detecting the movements of a seated user, the model exhibited limitations:

\begin{itemize}
\item Limited Posture Recognition: MoveNet struggled to accurately perceive the movements of a standing person with the same efficacy as those in a seated position. Extensive testing across various environments (lighting conditions, background variations) and user demographics (gender, height, skin tone) failed to yield significant improvements in this area.
\item Performance Issues with Speed: The model's processing speed proved to be a bottleneck, particularly when dealing with fast movements or movements performed at low angles. These situations resulted in stuttering, slowdowns, and instances of missed detections.
\end{itemize}

\noindent Due to these limitations, the project team decided to pursue alternative models. The primary focus shifted towards finding a model that offered:

\begin{itemize}
\item Lightweight Architecture: A smaller, more efficient model would improve processing speed and address the performance issues encountered with MoveNet.
\item Posture Agnostic Detection: The chosen model should be capable of accurately detecting and interpreting human movements regardless of the user's posture (standing or sitting).
\end{itemize}
By addressing these shortcomings, the team aimed to establish a more robust and versatile solution for capturing and interpreting user movements.


\subsection{Transmission of Motion Data to Digital Pipe Organ as MIDI Signal}

The identified movements will be mapped to corresponding MIDI messages. This mapping assigns specific movements to control various musical parameters like pitch, volume, and timbre (organ stop selection). \\
A software application will be developed to translate the interpreted movements into MIDI signals in real-time. This software will act as an intermediary, receiving movement data from the sensors and transmitting the corresponding MIDI messages to the digital pipe organ.
